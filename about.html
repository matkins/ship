About SHIP

The vision of SHIP is to see productive and sustainable health partnerships between the Sheffield area and low income countries.

Our mission

To facilitate changes beneficial to the health of people in the partnership countries.

Our aims

  1. To promote cross-cultural experience of health and health care by enabling staff from each end of the partnerships to observe alternative health practice.
  2. To increase morale and resilience of health staff by providing inspiration and encouragement from cross-cultural experience.
  3. To increase health awareness in the partnership communities* by communicating the health lessons learnt:
    1. through observation, reflection and feedback from cross-cultural exchanges
    2. through education and training.
  4. To engage the partnership populations in need by using, where appropriate, participatory needs assessments.
  5. To see resultant improvements in health and in health care arrangements at each end of the partnerships and to see these evaluated.

*People in need, service users, patients, carers and health staff.

Sheffield Health International Partnerships (SHIP) is a group of Sheffield health workers who are working with partners in Uganda. SHIP currently is comprised of two local organisations: Sheffield Medical Group - a Primary and Secondary Care partnership linked with Kumi, Eastern Uganda and the Sheffield Health and Social care NHS Trust - a Mental Health Partnership with Gulu in the North of Uganda. The two elements of SHIP work independently of each other but are closely linked and share a common philosophical approach to partnerships and to working in low income countries.

SHIP currently works in Uganda but may develop health links in other low income countries in future.

Mutual learning is at the heart of SHIP both in Uganda and in Sheffield - reflecting the spirit of partnership.
